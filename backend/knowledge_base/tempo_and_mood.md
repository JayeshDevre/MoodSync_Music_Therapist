# Tempo and Mood

## How BPM Affects Emotional State

Tempo (measured in beats per minute, BPM) is one of the strongest predictors of perceived emotional tone in music.

- **Fast tempo (120–180 BPM):** Associated with excitement, happiness, urgency, and high arousal states. Listeners report feeling energized and motivated. Ideal for workouts, celebration, or when someone needs a mood lift.
- **Medium tempo (90–120 BPM):** Neutral arousal zone. Works well for focus, casual listening, and social settings. Not too stimulating, not too sedating.
- **Slow tempo (60–90 BPM):** Associated with calm, sadness, reflection, and relaxation. Slows heart rate perception and reduces feelings of urgency. Ideal for winding down, studying, or processing difficult emotions.
- **Very slow tempo (below 60 BPM):** Deep relaxation, meditation, or melancholy. Can feel heavy or peaceful depending on other musical features.

## Tempo and Stress
Research in music therapy shows that listening to music with a tempo close to resting heart rate (60–80 BPM) can help regulate the nervous system and reduce perceived stress. This is why lofi and ambient music (typically 60–85 BPM) is popular for studying and anxiety relief.

## Tempo and Energy Matching
A key principle in music therapy is "iso principle" — matching music tempo to the listener's current emotional state first, then gradually shifting tempo to guide them toward a target state. For example, if someone is anxious (high arousal), starting with medium tempo and moving to slow is more effective than jumping straight to slow music.
