# Acousticness and Anxiety

## Why Acoustic Music Calms the Nervous System

Acoustic music (instruments without electronic amplification or heavy production) has a distinct effect on the human stress response compared to heavily produced electronic music.

- **Natural timbre:** Acoustic instruments like guitar, piano, and strings produce overtones that the brain processes as "natural" sounds, triggering lower threat responses compared to synthetic tones.
- **Dynamic variation:** Acoustic recordings have more natural volume variation (soft to loud), which keeps the brain gently engaged without overstimulating the nervous system.
- **Lower frequency density:** Acoustic music typically has fewer simultaneous frequency layers than electronic music, reducing cognitive load.

## Acousticness and Anxiety Relief
Studies in music therapy consistently show that high-acousticness music (score 0.7–1.0) is effective for:
- Reducing cortisol levels (the primary stress hormone)
- Lowering heart rate and blood pressure
- Helping with generalized anxiety disorder symptoms
- Improving sleep quality when listened to before bed

## When to Use High vs Low Acousticness
- **High acousticness (0.7–1.0):** Anxiety, stress, insomnia, grief, need for calm
- **Medium acousticness (0.4–0.7):** Neutral mood, casual focus, social settings
- **Low acousticness (0.0–0.4):** High energy needs, workouts, parties, euphoria

## Acoustic + Slow Tempo Combination
The most effective combination for anxiety relief is high acousticness paired with slow tempo (60–85 BPM). This combination is found in genres like folk, classical, lofi, and acoustic indie.
